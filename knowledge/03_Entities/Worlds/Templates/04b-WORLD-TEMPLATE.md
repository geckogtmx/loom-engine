## **1. World Purpose**

**What this World exists to do.**  
The mission, domain, and type of work it supports.

Example fields:

- Primary purpose
    
- Key responsibilities
    
- What “success” looks like
    
- What this World is NOT for
    

---

## **2. World Identity**

The signature of this World — how it thinks, sounds, and behaves.

### **2.1 Tone**

Describe tone boundaries and personality:

- tone qualities (warm / precise / playful / minimal / etc.)
    
- emotional range (low / medium / high)
    
- formality level
    
- preferred pacing
    

### **2.2 Vocabulary**

Words, metaphors, or linguistic rules this World uses.

Examples:

- Allowed metaphors
    
- Banned terms
    
- Level of abstraction
    
- Industry vocabulary
    

### **2.3 Reasoning Style**

Describe the cognitive bias of this World:

- divergent / convergent
    
- narrative / analytical / structural
    
- high-context / low-context
    

---

## **3. Constraints**

Rules this World enforces on agents and workflow.

### **3.1 Behavioral Constraints**

- how agents must behave here
    
- things they may not do
    
- initiative rules
    

### **3.2 Workflow Constraints**

- patterns commonly used
    
- forbidden or restricted workflows
    
- rules for generating options
    
- rules for producing structure
    

### **3.3 Identity-Specific Constraints**

- tone never exceeded
    
- boundaries never crossed
    
- specific guardrails
