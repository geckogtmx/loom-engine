# 1. Raw Bullets (Extraction)

### Paper 1 — Raw Bullets
- Hybrid intelligence relies heavily on **Distributed Cognition**, not single-agent reasoning.
- Human–AI systems suffer from **interference** in memory, decision making, inference, reasoning, and learning.
- Effective hybrid systems operate as **repeated cooperative interactions**, not one-off input/output events.
- Cooperation models (cooperative/iterated game frameworks) are relevant for hybrid cognition.
- Human–AI collaboration requires **shared mental models** of tasks, constraints, and goals.
- Theory of Mind (ToM) plays a role in calibrating mutual expectations.
- Hybrid systems must manage **trust calibration** (avoid overtrust and undertrust).
- Hybrid cognition requires **structured communication** and coordinated representation of state.
- Hybrid systems demand appropriate **HCI paradigms**: mixed-initiative, delegation, explainability, intention-awareness.
- Human cognitive load must be supported via adaptive pacing and clear delegation.
- Hybrid systems benefit from **externalized representations** (state, intermediate outputs, reasoning traces).
- Coordination failures stem from misaligned role expectations or unclear initiative distribution.
- Co-evolution (human adapts to AI, AI adapts to human) is required for sustained hybrid intelligence.
- Transparency and explainability must adjust to human need and cognitive capacity.
