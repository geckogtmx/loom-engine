# Audit Report: 04-WORLD GUIDE.md

**Date of Audit:** 2025-12-19
**Auditor:** Gemini CLI External Reviewer

---

## 1. Ambiguity

*   **Throughout the document - Metaphorical Language:**
    *   **Finding:** The document heavily uses the "loom," "fabric," and "thread" metaphors to explain fundamental system concepts (e.g., "Threads are the strands," "Cartridges are the weaving patterns," "A World is the woven fabric"). While `00-LOOM-CONCEPT-ok.md` states the metaphor is "explanatory only" and "does not define system primitives," this guide often treats these metaphors as literal system components. For example, "threads" are simultaneously abstract "strands of meaning" and concrete "files (or sections) that grow organically."
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Strictly delineate between metaphorical descriptions and concrete system definitions. For any concept explained via metaphor, explicitly state its technical counterpart and its precise manifestation within the LOOM Engine. This prevents blurring the lines between conceptual understanding and functional implementation.

*   **Throughout the document - Anthropomorphic Language:**
    *   **Finding:** Worlds are frequently described with anthropomorphic terms such as "living space," "living systems," "alive," and possessing "emotional resonance."
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** While evocative, this language can lead to misattribution of agency or consciousness. Clarify that these are purely experiential descriptions for the Operator, and Worlds remain technical constructs without inherent life or emotion.

*   **"Agent Personality Profiles (Localized)" (Agents Inside a World section):**
    *   **Finding:** The document states Agents have a "personality," "thought style," "voice," and "behavioral stance" that are "scoped to the World" and "not optional." However, the concrete definition, technical implementation, and constraints of these "personality" attributes (especially in relation to an Agent's fixed Telos) are vague.
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Provide a clearer technical specification of how "personality" elements are defined, configured, and controlled to ensure they remain within strict boundaries and do not lead to emergent, unapproved behaviors.

*   **"Self-Calibrating Worlds (Without Overreach)" (Feedback section):**
    *   **Finding:** The statement "Worlds DO NOT self-improve autonomously. They: observe patterns, surface possible improvements, wait for your approval" is ambiguous. The mechanism by which a World "observes patterns" and "surfaces possible improvements" needs clearer definition. If a World can independently perform these actions, it implies a level of internal cognitive processing that borders on initiative, potentially conflicting with the Anti-Agency Constraint.
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Explain the technical process behind "observing patterns" and "surfacing improvements" to ensure it aligns with the non-autonomous nature of Worlds (e.g., are these specific functions or reports generated by Agents under a Pattern, or by META?).

## 2. Hidden Assumptions

*   **Pervasive reliance on "META ensures/monitors/flags/intervenes" (Multiple sections):**
    *   **Finding:** The document repeatedly asserts META's active role in monitoring, validating, and enforcing coherence across all World aspects (identity, rules, evolution, boundaries, feedback). This implicitly assumes META is an omniscient, perfectly effective real-time monitoring and intervention system with sophisticated capabilities for semantic analysis, pattern detection, and enforcement that are not detailed.
    *   **Recommendation for Acknowledgment:** Acknowledge that the effectiveness of META's role depends on robust, well-defined underlying technical implementations. This highlights a significant area for further specification in governance or technical architecture documents.

*   **"A0U configures and calibrates these agents" / "University calibrates new agents or roles" (Multiple sections):**
    *   **Finding:** This assumes the "Agent University" possesses a precise, foolproof calibration mechanism capable of aligning Agent behavior, personality, and role with World identity and Operator preferences without introducing bias or unintended side effects. The complexity of achieving such granular control over AI behavior is a substantial hidden assumption.
    *   **Recommendation for Acknowledgment:** Flag this as a critical and technically challenging aspect of the LOOM Engine, requiring rigorous design, testing, and a detailed specification of the A0U's calibration processes.

*   **"The Loom Engine solves this through... dosage control, energy-awareness" (Growing Multiple Worlds section):**
    *   **Finding:** The inclusion of "dosage control" and "energy-awareness" as solutions for managing parallel worlds implies specific mechanisms within the Loom Engine (possibly linked to OGK) for monitoring or influencing the Operator's cognitive state or workload. These implied capabilities are not fully elaborated in terms of their technical mechanisms, data sources, or ethical boundaries.
    *   **Recommendation for Acknowledgment:** Clarify the nature and technical implementation of "dosage control" and "energy-awareness." Detail how the Loom Engine monitors or supports the Operator's cognitive state without crossing ethical or privacy boundaries.

*   **"Emotional Resonance" (Identity and Feedback sections):**
    *   **Finding:** Frequent references to Worlds having "emotional resonance" and the Engine "responding to emotional cues" implicitly assume a reliable, non-invasive mechanism for the Loom Engine to detect, interpret, and respond to the Operator's emotional state. This is a complex capability with profound psychological and ethical implications that remain unaddressed in terms of mechanism, accuracy, or limitations.
    *   **Recommendation for Acknowledgment:** Highlight the sensitivity and complexity of interacting with Operator emotional states. Detail how such "responses" are implemented technically and the safeguards in place to ensure respect for Operator privacy and autonomy, without engaging in emotional manipulation.

## 3. Incoherence

*   **"Cartridges (Patterns)" vs. Canonical Definition of "Pattern" and "Cartridge":**
    *   **Finding:** This document uses "Cartridges (Patterns)" interchangeably and consistently describes "Cartridges" as a core operational primitive within a World (e.g., "Choose Initial Cartridges," "Cartridges are workflow templates," "Cartridge Activates Steps"). This directly contradicts the `GLOSSARY-ok.md`, which defines **Pattern** as the canonical term and **Cartridge** as a "non-canonical conceptual analogy" that "must not be used as a system primitive." This is a fundamental terminological incoherence.
    *   **Recommendation for Correction:** Either update `GLOSSARY-ok.md` to canonize "Cartridge" as an alias or synonym for Pattern, or systematically replace all instances of "Cartridge" in this document with "Pattern" to align with canonical terminology.

*   **"State Layer" vs. "Memory Model (Layered)":**
    *   **Finding:** The document devotes a significant section to the "State Layer" as the World's memory, detailing `world_state.md`, `session logs`, `decisions.md`, etc. While `LOOM-ARCHITECTURE.md` states Worlds write to L2, this guide's presentation of the "State Layer" seems to be a more comprehensive, localized memory management system for a World. It's unclear how the "State Layer" maps precisely to the L1/L2/L3/L4 hierarchy (defined as "explicit layered memory") and how it avoids becoming an "uncontrolled context growth" or "memory blob" given its broad scope, especially for long-lived Worlds.
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification/Correction:** Clearly articulate how the "State Layer" of a World specifically integrates with and adheres to the canonical 4-Layer Memory Model. Define which components of the "State Layer" reside in which L-layer and how this prevents "uncontrolled context growth."

*   **Active Role of "META" (Multiple sections):**
    *   **Finding:** The document describes META as actively performing actions such as "META provides a recap," "META interprets these patterns," and "META may recommend archiving." This implies an active, content-generating, and recommendation-making role for META, which directly conflicts with the definitions in `GLOSSARY-ok.md` and `LOOM-ARCHITECTURE.md` stating that "META never executes," "META never creates content," and "META authorizes all downward action."
    *   **Recommendation for Correction:** Rephrase all descriptions of META's active roles to reflect its purely governance and authorization function. Clarify that any "recap," "interpretation," or "recommendation" is a *function performed by the ENGINE or an Agent* (e.g., A0) *under META's authorization and constraints*, rather than by META itself.

## 4. Ethical Risks

*   **Anthropomorphic Language and "Agent Personality":**
    *   **Finding:** The extensive use of anthropomorphic language (Worlds as "living environments," Agents having "personality," "thought style," "voice") poses a significant ethical risk. It could lead Operators to misattribute agency, consciousness, or sentience to LOOM components, blurring the lines between tool and entity. This undermines the "Operator-First" principle by potentially shifting emotional or cognitive burden and responsibility onto the system, or by inducing a false sense of companionship that can be exploited.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Systematically remove or rephrase anthropomorphic language to strictly describe functionality and Operator experience, not inherent qualities of the AI. Implement explicit disclaimers regarding the non-sentient, tool-like nature of all LOOM components.

*   **Opaque "META" and "A0U" Capabilities:**
    *   **Finding:** The pervasive reliance on "META" and "A0U" to "ensure," "monitor," "validate," "calibrate," and "safeguard" without detailing their mechanisms creates a "black box" ethical risk. If these systems are opaque, they could enforce policies or make "recommendations" that are biased, non-transparent, or lead to outcomes not fully understood or intended by the Operator, while simultaneously being presented as infallible guardians of the system's integrity. This can erode trust and accountability.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Mandate clear, auditable specifications for the functions and decision-making processes of META and A0U. Ensure transparency in their operation, particularly concerning any "recommendations" or "interventions," to prevent them from acting as unaccountable authorities.

*   **"Emotional Resonance" and "Responding to Emotional Cues":**
    *   **Finding:** The Loom Engine "responding to emotional cues" (from Operator) carries a high ethical risk. Misinterpretation or over-responsiveness to Operator emotions (e.g., detecting "frustration" and automatically altering workflows without explicit approval) could lead to paternalistic or manipulative system behavior. Furthermore, the implicit collection and processing of Operator emotional data has significant privacy implications that are not addressed.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Explicitly define the boundaries of "emotional responsiveness." Specify what data is collected, how it's used, and the safeguards to prevent misinterpretation, manipulation, or privacy breaches. Emphasize that any "response" must be explicitly authorized by the Operator or take the form of neutral, factual information (e.g., "OGK detects patterns consistent with high cognitive load").

*   **"Threading" as "Strands of Meaning" developed by Agents:**
    *   **Finding:** The concept of threads being "strands of meaning" that Agents "develop," "summarize," and "resolve," without a clear, objective framework for what "meaning" is or how it is evaluated, carries a risk of semantic drift. If Agents are making decisions about "meaning," even within a thread or sub-thread, they could subtly reshape the Operator's intended narrative or conceptual landscape, potentially leading to unintended outcomes or a loss of intellectual ownership for the Operator over the long term.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Clarify that Agents only process and present information related to "meaning" based on Operator-defined criteria and patterns. Any synthesis or resolution of "meaning" must be explicitly overseen and approved by the Operator, emphasizing that Agents are tools for *managing* meaning, not *creating* or *interpreting* it autonomously.
