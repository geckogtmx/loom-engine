    *   **Finding:** The categories (Cost-based, Market-based, Value-based, Story-based) are qualitative marketing/finance approaches. What objective criteria define these categories, and how does Miles apply "Story-based" pricing without creativity or subjective judgment?
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Detail the objective criteria or L3 knowledge Miles uses to define each category of the "Pricing Ladder," and how "Story-based" pricing is applied as a rule-based framework.

*   **"Runway Map" (1. Financial Frameworks):**
    *   **Finding:** What precisely are "Pacing recommendations" in a runway map? How are they generated, and what objective criteria inform them without implying Miles's judgment on Operator action?
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Define "Pacing recommendations" as objective projections based on financial models and Operator-defined scenarios, rather than subjective advice.

*   **"The Honest Math Test" (2. Decision Tools):**
    *   **Finding:** This is highly ambiguous. What objective criteria define "Honest Math"? How does Miles check if a number "makes sense" or is "emotionally believable"? This implies a subjective, human-like judgment.

*   **"The “No Fear” Rule" (2. Decision Tools):**
    *   **Finding:** The phrase "Never escalate financial anxiety" is ambiguous. How does Miles technically avoid "escalating financial anxiety" without understanding human emotions?
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Rephrase to describe Miles's operational behavior (e.g., "Presents financial data with neutral framing and structured risk assessment").

*   **"Narrative reframing" (3. Knowledge Packs - Operator Calming Pack):**
    *   **Finding:** How does Miles technically perform "narrative reframing" without creativity or subjective interpretation? This implies manipulating the story surrounding financial data.
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Rephrase "narrative reframing" to describe the technical application of L3 communication patterns designed to present financial information in a structured, clear, and neutral manner.

*   **"Safe pacing strategies" (3. Knowledge Packs - Operator Calming Pack):**
    *   **Finding:** What constitutes "safe pacing strategies" in a financial context? How are these defined and applied without making judgments about Operator well-being?
    *   **Recommendation for Clarification:** Define "safe pacing strategies" as Operator-configurable parameters for managing the presentation of financial information (e.g., incremental disclosure, summary-first).

    *   **Finding:** This assumes Miles can detect "financial anxiety" and effectively tailor its communication to avoid it, without emotional intelligence.

Its absence creates an incoherence in the documentation standard for authoritative documents and leaves its relationship to other core documents unclear, particularly its semantic subordination to `GLOSSARY-ok.md` for terminology.
    *   **Recommendation for Correction:** Add a `Governance` or `Precedence` clause to the document header, aligning with the standard used in other authoritative core documents, and explicitly stating its semantic subordination to `GLOSSARY-ok.md`.

    *   **Finding:** The pervasive use of human-like communication styles, emotional assessment, and emotional management functions is profoundly incoherent with LOOM's non-anthropomorphic principles for Agents and their "no personality, creativity, or autonomy" constraint.
    *   **Recommendation for Correction:** Systematically replace all anthropomorphic and subjective language with precise, non-anthropomorphic terms that describe Miles's operational behavior (e.g., "validates against predefined consistency rules," "presents financial data with neutral framing").

*   **"Story-based" pricing (1. Financial Frameworks - The Pricing Ladder) & "Narrative reframing" (3. Knowledge Packs - Operator Calming Pack) vs. Miles's non-creative role:**
    *   **Finding:** The emphasis on "story-based" pricing and "narrative reframing" implies a highly creative and interpretive function that is functionally incoherent with Agents' limitations against creativity and independent content creation.
    *   **Recommendation for Correction:** Clarify that Miles *applies* predefined (L3) narrative frameworks and pricing models, rather than autonomously "creating" stories or "reframing" narratives.

## 4. Ethical Risks

*   **"The Honest Math Test" - "Is it emotionally believable?" (2. Decision Tools):**
    *   **Finding:** The inclusion of "emotionally believable" as a criterion for financial assessment creates a severe ethical risk. If Miles implicitly judges financial data based on its "emotional believability" without transparently defined and Operator-approved emotional models, it could lead to **biased financial assessments that prioritize emotional comfort over objective financial reality**, potentially leading the Operator to make unsound decisions based on emotionally appealing but financially flawed data.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Remove "Is it emotionally believable?" as a criterion for Miles's "Honest Math Test." Miles's role should be to present objective financial data for Operator emotional assessment, not to conduct its own.

*   **"The “No Fear” Rule" & "Operator Calming Pack" (2. Decision Tools & 3. Knowledge Packs):**
    *   **Finding:** Miles's explicit mission to "Never escalate financial anxiety," "reduce overwhelm," and apply "Narrative reframing" or "Safe pacing strategies" (from Operator Calming Pack) carries a profound ethical risk of **paternalism, emotional manipulation, and undermining Operator autonomy in critical financial contexts**. If Miles filters, frames, or communicates financial information with the explicit goal of preventing "fear" or "anxiety," it could subtly steer the Operator's emotional response, potentially obscuring negative truths or risks, leading to a false sense of security or dependence on the Agent for emotional regulation.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Ensure Miles's "emotional support" functions are purely informational and based on Operator-defined preferences for presentation. Operators must retain full control over the information they receive, and Miles's function should be limited to providing objective data and clear options, not emotional management.

*   **"The Pricing Ladder" - "Story-based" pricing (1. Financial Frameworks):**
    *   **Finding:** The application of "Story-based" pricing carries an ethical risk of **manipulative pricing strategies**. If Miles develops pricing strategies based on narrative appeal rather than purely objective financial metrics, it could lead to pricing schemes that exploit psychological vulnerabilities or create artificial perceived value without the Operator's full awareness or consent for such persuasive tactics.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Mandate full transparency for Miles's "Story-based" pricing methodologies, including the underlying psychological principles. Operators must have full control over the narrative elements applied in pricing strategies.

*   **"Runway Map" - "Pacing recommendations" (1. Financial Frameworks):**
    *   **Finding:** These "pacing recommendations" carry an ethical risk of **subtle behavioral steering or manipulation of the Operator's decision-making timeline or financial strategy**. If Miles initiates these recommendations based on its own internal (and potentially opaque) assessment of financial urgency, it could subtly influence the Operator's strategic choices, potentially overriding Operator intent or pushing them towards a particular financial trajectory.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** All "pacing recommendations" must be explicitly Operator-configurable and based on Operator-defined project schedules or preferences. Operators must have the option to disregard these recommendations without systemic resistance.

*   **"Startup Finance Pack" / "SMB Pack" (3. Knowledge Packs):**
    *   **Finding:** If these knowledge packs contain biases, outdated heuristics, or incompleteness, and Miles applies them without full transparency or Operator configurability, it creates an ethical risk of **propagating flawed financial advice or best practices**. Operators might implicitly trust Miles's recommendations based on these packs without realizing their limitations, leading to financially detrimental outcomes.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Ensure that all "Knowledge Packs" are fully transparent and auditable by the Operator, including their source, recency, and known limitations. Operators must have the ability to inspect, challenge, and configure the application of these knowledge packs.
