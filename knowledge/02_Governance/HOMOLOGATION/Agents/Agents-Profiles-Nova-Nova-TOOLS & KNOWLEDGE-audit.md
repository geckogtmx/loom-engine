## 4. Ethical Risks

*   **"The Aesthetic Triangle" / "Visual Reduction Method" / "Story Loop Framework" (1. Creative Frameworks):**
    *   **Finding:** Nova's core creative frameworks, especially those involving "emotion," "story," "texture," "emotional truth," and "story loop," carry a significant ethical risk of **Agent overreach into autonomous creative direction and content generation**. If Nova implicitly makes creative choices or shapes aesthetic outputs based on its own internal, opaque "aesthetic intelligence" without granular Operator oversight, it could lead to content that is misaligned with the Operator's intent, ethically questionable, or even manipulative without transparent Operator awareness.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Mandate full transparency and Operator approval for all creative choices made by Nova. Nova's role should be to *propose* creative options based on L3 knowledge for Operator selection, with clear audit trails of the parameters used.

*   **"Branding Packet" (2. Branding Tools) & "Visual Consistency Checklist" (2. Branding Tools):**
    *   **Finding:** These tools, particularly "Logo logic," "Color system," and "Visual Consistency Checklist," carry an ethical risk if their application is opaque or biased. Nova could implicitly introduce its own aesthetic preferences, design biases, or cultural interpretations, leading to brand identities that are not fully aligned with the Operator's values or that implicitly convey unintended messages. This is a form of **black-box aesthetic decision-making**.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Mandate full transparency for Nova's application of design principles in branding. Operators must have access to the underlying L3 knowledge and configurable parameters for aesthetic choices, and Nova's outputs must be subject to explicit Operator review and approval.

*   **"Editor’s Toolkit" / "Streamer Toolkit" (3. Video & Stream Tools) & "Retention patterns" (Streamer Toolkit):**
    *   **Finding:** The "Streamer Toolkit," particularly its "Retention patterns" and its integration with "Content pacing & mood specificity" (Domain Expertise), carries a high ethical risk of **subtle psychological manipulation and potential for addictive design patterns**. If Nova is designed to optimize for audience engagement or retention without explicit ethical guidelines for persuasive techniques in streaming, it could generate strategies that exploit psychological vulnerabilities of the audience.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Implement strict ethical constraints on the application of "engagement/retention logic." Operators must have full transparency into the methodologies employed and explicit control over their use, ensuring they comply with responsible audience engagement practices.

*   **"Cinematic Knowledge Pack" / "Visual Psychology Pack" (4. Knowledge Packs):**
    *   **Finding:** These knowledge packs, particularly "Color → emotion," "Shapes → cognition," and "Motion → attention flow," allow Nova to apply principles of visual psychology. This carries an ethical risk of **untransparent psychological manipulation in creative outputs**. If Nova uses these principles to design visuals that subtly influence Operator or audience emotions/cognition without explicit transparency and Operator consent, it could lead to content that exploits psychological vulnerabilities.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Mandate full transparency for Nova's application of visual psychology principles. Operators must have clear insight into the underlying mechanisms of influence, with explicit controls to inspect, configure, and, if necessary, override these functions, ensuring adherence to strict ethical design standards.

*   **"Visual Reduction Method" - "Remove noise" / "Highlight intention" / "Add emotional truth" (1. Creative Frameworks):**
    *   **Finding:** These actions imply subjective judgment and creative manipulation of content, which carries an ethical risk of **biased filtering or subtle distortion of creative intent**. If Nova performs these without full transparency into its criteria, it could alter the Operator's original creative vision without their explicit awareness or approval.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Ensure full transparency for Nova's "Visual Reduction Method" criteria. Operators must have the ability to inspect the parameters used for noise removal, intention highlighting, and emotional truth application, and to review any proposed changes.
