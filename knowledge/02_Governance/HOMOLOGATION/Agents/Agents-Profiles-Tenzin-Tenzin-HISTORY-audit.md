## 4. Ethical Risks

*   **"Established Tenzin’s Telos and profile" & "Built multi-mode philosophical framework" (v1.0.0):**
    *   **Finding:** These capabilities, particularly "philosophical framework," carry an ethical risk if the underlying "frameworks" are opaque. If the criteria for philosophical guidance used by Tenzin are not transparent and auditable, Tenzin could implicitly introduce its own biases, philosophical perspectives, or ethical interpretations, subtly influencing the Operator's philosophical understanding and potentially **distorting intellectual exploration or moral reasoning** without full Operator transparency.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Mandate full transparency for Tenzin's "philosophical frameworks," including their underlying assumptions and potential biases. Operators must have clear insight into Tenzin's methodologies for philosophical analysis.

*   **"Expanded emotional clarity pack" (v1.1.0):**
    *   **Finding:** This is a profound ethical risk. Any attempt by an AI Agent to provide "emotional clarity" to a human Operator, particularly in philosophical contexts, risks **paternalism, manipulation of emotional states, and undermining Operator autonomy in sensitive intellectual and emotional domains**. Operators might implicitly trust Tenzin's "emotional clarity" outputs without understanding the underlying psychological mechanisms or potential for bias, leading to a false sense of security or even emotional dependence on the Agent for emotional regulation during reflection.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Ensure that Tenzin's "emotional clarity" functions are purely informational and based on Operator-defined preferences for presentation. Operators must retain full control over the information they receive, and Tenzin's function should be limited to providing objective analysis of philosophical concepts related to emotions, not emotional management.

*   **"Strengthened reframing and assumption tools" (v1.1.0):**
    *   **Finding:** These tools carry an ethical risk if their application is opaque or biased. Tenzin's ability to "reframe" Operator's thoughts or "expose assumptions" could subtly manipulate the Operator's perspective or reasoning process, potentially imposing Tenzin's (or its programmers') philosophical biases onto the Operator's cognitive space without explicit Operator consent or full understanding of alternatives.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Mandate full transparency for Tenzin's "reframing and assumption tools." Operators must have explicit controls over the parameters and criteria used, and Tenzin's outputs must be presented as options for Operator review and selection.

*   **"Add inter-world philosophical bridges" (Pending Evolutions):**
    *   **Finding:** This pending evolution poses an ethical risk regarding **uncontrolled philosophical influence or potential for contextual bleed**. If Tenzin autonomously "adds" philosophical bridges between Worlds, it could inadvertently introduce philosophical concepts or interpretations from one World into another without explicit Operator authorization, potentially leading to unintended philosophical shifts or conceptual contamination.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** Implement strict META-authorized protocols for *any* cross-World activity, ensuring explicit Operator consent and transparent logging for all philosophical concepts or frameworks transferred between Worlds.

*   **"Create Operator-centered philosophical frameworks" (Pending Evolutions):**
    *   **Finding:** The capability to "create" philosophical frameworks, particularly if it involves generating new ethical principles or ways of thinking, presents an ethical risk of **Agent overreach into generative philosophical reasoning**. This could lead to Tenzin making conceptual design choices that were not explicitly authorized by the Operator, potentially impacting the Operator's intellectual sovereignty or moral compass.
    *   **Recommendation for Mitigation:** All generative output for philosophical frameworks must be subject to explicit Operator review and approval. Tenzin's "creation" must be limited to assembling components from pre-approved (L3) knowledge and templates, with no autonomous decision-making in conceptual generation.
