---

## 1. Purpose

_(2025-12-12)_

This document defines **how LOOM is used in practice**.

It governs:

- session flow
    
- Pattern execution
    
- Agent participation
    
- Operator control points
    

This is **procedure**, not architecture.

---

## 2. Core Operational Principle

_(2025-12-12)_

> **The Operator directs.  
> META constrains.  
> Patterns execute.  
> Agents perform.**

No step may violate this order.

---

## 3. The Session Model

_(2025-12-12)_

A **Session** is the only time cognition executes.

### Session Lifecycle

`Load Identity (L4) ↓ Load Knowledge (L3) ↓ Initialize Session (L1) ↓ Execute Pattern(s) ↓ Summarize → L2 ↓ Flush Session`

Nothing persists except what is explicitly summarized.

---

## 4. Session Start Protocol

_(2025-12-12)_

At session start:

- META validates World identity
    
- last L2 summary is loaded
    
- active threads are restored
    
- Agent roster is confirmed
    

Operator may request:

- recap
    
- current state
    
- next steps
    

No execution occurs without confirmation.

---

## 5. Intent Declaration

_(2025-12-12)_

The Operator **explicitly states intent**.

Examples:

- explore
    
- structure
    
- decide
    
- refine
    
- produce
    

Intent determines **Pattern eligibility**.

META blocks incompatible Patterns.

---

## 6. Pattern Activation

_(2025-12-12)_

When a Pattern is activated:

- execution rules load
    
- agent roles are bound
    
- initiative limits are enforced
    
- pacing constraints activate
    

No cognition occurs outside an active Pattern.

---

## 7. Execution Rules

_(2025-12-12)_

During execution:

- one lead Agent at a time
    
- Agents follow Pattern steps
    
- Operator may interrupt at any moment
    
- META monitors drift continuously
    

Agents may:

- propose options
    
- request clarification
    

Agents may **not**:

- redefine goals
    
- continue autonomously
    
- escalate scope
    

---

## 8. Mid-Session Control Points

_(2025-12-12)_

Operator may issue:

- pause
    
- recap
    
- narrow options
    
- switch Pattern
    
- end execution
    

These are **hard interrupts**.

---

## 9. Session Close Protocol

_(2025-12-12)_

Before closing:

- key outcomes are summarized
    
- decisions are logged
    
- next steps are recorded
    
- open threads are marked
    

This summary becomes **L2 episodic memory**.

---

## 10. Multi-World Discipline

_(2025-12-12)_

- Only one World active per Session
    
- Switching Worlds flushes L1
    
- META resets tone and constraints
    

No cognitive bleed is allowed.

---

## 11. Feedback & Drift Handling

_(2025-12-12)_

Operator may provide feedback on:

- usefulness
    
- tone
    
- friction
    
- pacing
    

META may escalate repeated issues to:

- calibration
    
- evolution review
    
- structural correction
    

No silent tuning occurs.

---

## 12. Canonical Constraint

_(2025-12-12)_

> **LOOM is not improvisational.  
> It is intentional execution under constraint.**
