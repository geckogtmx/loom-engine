# **08-FEEDBACK-LOOP.md**

_Operator-Led Micro-Evolution System for the Loom Engine_  
_How the Engine learns, adjusts, and improves safely over time._

---

## **1. Purpose of the Feedback Loop**

The Loom Engine does **not** learn autonomously.  
It evolves **only** through structured Operator feedback, filtered and controlled by META and executed by A0U + University.

The Feedback Loop is how the Engine:

- corrects misalignments
    
- refines agent behavior
    
- stabilizes tone & identity
    
- improves workflow comfort
    
- prevents drift
    
- supports Operator growth
    

It is the **adaptive mechanism** that keeps long-term collaboration predictable and aligned.

The Loop must always respect the Operator Telos — identity, values, constraints, rhythm, preferences, and long-arc vision.

---

## **2. The Feedback Cycle Overview**

Every session ends with an optional but highly recommended cycle:

1. **Operator Exit Poll**
    
2. **META Filtering**
    
3. **University Micro-Adjustments**
    
4. **A0U & World Update**
    
5. **Continuity Logging**
    

This cycle ensures the Engine improves **without drift** and stays aligned with the Operator’s identity and preferences.

---

## **3. Stage 1 — Operator Exit Poll**

A lightweight reflective moment where the Operator answers (informally or explicitly):

### **3.1 What Worked**

- Agent tone
    
- Clarity of structure
    
- Pace and initiative
    
- Pattern choice
    
- Collaboration feel
    

### **3.2 What Didn’t**

- overwhelming output
    
- tone mismatch
    
- unclear workflow steps
    
- too rigid / too loose reasoning
    
- energy mismatch
    

### **3.3 Desired Adjustments**

Examples:

- “Tone should be softer next time.”
    
- “Strategist should ask more clarifying questions.”
    
- “Writer drifted into over-metaphor; tighten constraints.”
    
- “Lower initiative during refinement.”
    

### **3.4 Session Context**

Optional tags:

- energy level
    
- emotional load
    
- cognitive state
    

This helps refine cognitive rhythm support in future sessions.  
(See: Cognitive Rhythm section in Operator Telos.)

---

## **4. Stage 2 — META Division Filtering**

META receives Operator feedback and categorizes items into:

### **4.1 Structural Issues**

- Pattern mismatch
    
- Workflow inconsistencies
    
- World rule misalignment
    
- identity friction
    

### **4.2 Agent Behavior Issues**

- tone drift
    
- initiative misfires
    
- unclear reasoning
    
- role confusion
    

(Refer to Agent Constraints.)

### **4.3 Pattern Issues**

- steps unclear or too heavy
    
- reasoning mode misaligned
    
- transitions too abrupt
    

### **4.4 Alignment Concerns**

Anything violating:

- Operator values
    
- constraints
    
- boundaries
    
- communication preferences
    

(From the Telos.)

### **4.5 Noise / Discarded Items**

META discards:

- contradictory feedback
    
- out-of-scope requests
    
- changes that would break structure
    
- feedback based on low-energy distortions
    
- flavor preferences not aligned with identity
    

Filtering ensures the system **does not mutate chaotically**.

---

## **5. Stage 3 — University Micro-Adjustments**

The University applies targeted calibrations to affected agents.

### **5.1 Tone Adjustments**

Examples:

- soften language
    
- make more minimal
    
- reduce metaphor density
    
- increase clarity
    

### **5.2 Reasoning Style Tuning**

Examples:

- more analytical
    
- more narrative
    
- more structural
    
- slower pace
    
- deeper or shallower divergence
    

### **5.3 Initiative Level Adjustment**

Adjust based on drift or Operator comfort.  
(See: Initiative system.)

### **5.4 Collaboration Pathway Fixes**

Examples:

- clarify handoffs
    
- reduce overlap
    
- slow down the lead agent
    
- enforce sequential outputs
    

### **5.5 Constraint Reinforcement**

If an agent violated a constraint:

- reminder of role
    
- tightened guardrails
    
- new boundary checks
    

This keeps the system safe and predictable.

---

## **6. Stage 4 — A0U & World Update**

After University adjustments, A0U updates:

### **6.1 Agent Configuration Files**

- role notes
    
- constraint definitions
    
- tone anchors
    
- reasoning defaults
    

(This is stored in the World’s configuration layer.)

### **6.2 Pattern References**

If session friction came from workflow structure:

- suggest new Pattern
    
- refine Pattern usage rules
    
- adjust activation conditions
    

### **6.3 world_state Update**

Includes:

- decisions
    
- insight logs
    
- behavioral notes
    
- next steps
    
- adjustments for next session
    

This creates multi-session continuity (World Layer + State Layer).

---

## **7. Stage 5 — Continuity Logging**

META writes a **micro-log** that:

- records adjustments
    
- marks drift corrections
    
- preserves identity continuity
    
- prevents future misalignment
    

This reinforces the “memory through structure, not storage” approach.

The Growth Kernel may also detect long-term patterns here.  
(See OGK, Architecture file.)

---

## **8. Types of Feedback the Engine Can Use**

### **8.1 Usable Feedback**

- tone corrections
    
- pace adjustments
    
- structural clarity issues
    
- initiative adjustments
    
- workflow friction
    
- cross-agent coordination
    
- Pattern fit/misfit
    
- energy alignment issues
    

### **8.2 Feedback Requiring Operator Approval**

- major role changes
    
- new agents creation
    
- personality rewrites
    
- new World identity
    
- updates to Operator Telos
    

META escalates these before implementation.

### **8.3 Feedback the Engine Will Reject**

- contradictory preferences
    
- changes that violate values/constraints
    
- requests that break World identity
    
- unrealistic cross-domain jumps
    
- unsafe autonomy changes
    

The system prioritizes _Operator alignment_ and _structural stability_ above all.

---

## **10. Feedback Loop Timing**

### **Per Session (Recommended)**

Quick feedback prevents drift from accumulating.

### **Per Day (Optional)**

Useful when switching Worlds frequently.

### **Per Milestone (Strategic Worlds)**

Podcast episodes, product chapters, design cycles.

### **Per World Evolution Phase**

When identity, purpose, or rules change.

---

## **11. Safety & Constraints of the Feedback Loop**

The Loop **cannot** override:

- Operator Telos
    
- World identity
    
- structural rules
    
- META guardrails
    
- agent constraints
    

Any adjustment requiring a major identity shift must be reviewed by the Operator first.

This mechanism ensures:

- no runaway learning
    
- no personality distortion
    
- no chaotic system mutation
    

All evolution is **Operator-led** and **META-protected**.

---

The Loop is the **central adaptation system** enabling:

- stable long-running Worlds
    
- consistent agent behavior
    
- personal alignment
    
- reduced friction
    
- improved collaboration over time
    
- emotional + cognitive fit
    
- continuity across months or years
    

In short:

> **The Feedback Loop is how the Loom Engine _learns you_ without ever storing your personal data.**

It evolves through structure — not memory.
