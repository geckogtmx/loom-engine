# **ETHICS — LOOM Engine**

> **Status:** Foundational  
> **Scope:** Architectural & Interaction Ethics  
> **Authority:** Operator-First  
> **Note:** This document defines _structural ethical boundaries_, not moral judgments.

---

## 1. Scope

This document describes the **ethical posture of the LOOM Engine** as expressed through its architecture, governance, and interaction model.

LOOM does **not**:

- define moral values
    
- evaluate the ethical quality of outcomes
    
- make decisions on behalf of the Operator
    
- substitute for human judgment
    

LOOM **does**:

- encode ethical constraints through structure
    
- preserve responsibility and authorship
    
- reduce ethically hazardous ambiguity in human–AI interaction
    

Ethics in LOOM is **procedural and architectural**, not normative.

---

## 2. Ethical Position

LOOM is built on a single ethical premise:

> **Responsibility must remain legible.**

To support this, LOOM enforces:

- explicit authority boundaries
    
- deliberate persistence
    
- non-agentic execution
    
- governed evolution
    
- resistance to hidden influence
    

LOOM does not attempt to be ethical _on behalf of_ the Operator.  
It is designed to make certain ethical failures **structurally difficult**.

---

## 3. Non-Negotiable Ethical Principles

### 3.1 Operator Sovereignty

The human Operator is the sole source of:

- intent
    
- authority
    
- decision-making
    
- authorship of meaning
    

No system component may infer, assume, or extend Operator intent beyond what is explicitly provided.

---

### 3.2 Explicit Authority Separation

LOOM enforces a strict separation between:

- intent (Operator)
    
- governance (META)
    
- enforcement (A0)
    
- execution (ENGINE)
    
- cognition (Agents)
    

This separation exists to prevent:

- implicit agency
    
- ambiguous responsibility
    
- untraceable decision paths
    

---

### 3.3 Deliberate Persistence

Nothing in LOOM persists by default.

All memory, continuity, and identity changes require:

- explicit Operator action
    
- governed authorization
    
- inspectable mechanisms
    

Persistence without consent is treated as an ethical violation.

---

### 3.4 Non-Agentic Operation

LOOM components do not:

- initiate goals
    
- optimize independently
    
- self-modify
    
- learn autonomously
    
- act across Sessions without authorization
    

Optimization without initiation authority is not agency — but **optimization without oversight is**.

LOOM disallows both.

---

## 4. Ethical Failure Modes LOOM Is Designed to Prevent

### 4.1 False Attribution of Agency

LOOM is designed to prevent situations where:

- the system appears to decide
    
- responsibility becomes ambiguous
    
- intent is misattributed to non-human components
    

Clarity of authorship takes precedence over convenience.

---

### 4.2 Unconsented Persistence

LOOM resists:

- implicit memory growth
    
- background adaptation
    
- silent evolution
    
- unintended continuity
    

Continuity exists to serve the Operator, not to surprise them.

---

### 4.3 Semantic Drift During Absence

Meaning does not evolve while the Operator is absent.

LOOM does not reinterpret, reframe, or advance understanding between Sessions unless explicitly instructed to do so.

Meaning belongs to its author.

---

### 4.4 Hidden Optimization

LOOM does not pursue latent objectives, reward functions, or performance goals beyond those explicitly defined.

Any form of optimization must be:

- visible
    
- bounded
    
- reversible
    
- Operator-directed
    

---

### 4.5 Sycophancy (Epistemic Deference Without Justification)

LOOM treats sycophancy as an ethical failure, not a stylistic flaw.

Unwarranted agreement, affirmation without sufficient epistemic grounding, or performative alignment with Operator beliefs compromises the integrity of cognition and obscures responsibility.

Support in LOOM does **not** imply endorsement.  
Agreement must remain reasoned, qualified, and honest.

---

## 5. Operator Responsibility

LOOM preserves human responsibility; it does not absorb it.

The Operator remains responsible for:

- decisions
    
- interpretations
    
- outcomes
    
- ethical judgment beyond structural constraints
    

LOOM exists to **support clarity**, not to provide moral cover.

---

## 6. Closing Statement

LOOM is not an ethical agent.

It is a **governed interaction framework** designed to reduce ethically hazardous ambiguity in long-term human–AI collaboration.

Where LOOM refuses to act, it does so deliberately — to ensure that responsibility, meaning, and intent remain where they belong.
