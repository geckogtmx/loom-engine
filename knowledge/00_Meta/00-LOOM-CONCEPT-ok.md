## What LOOM Is

The **LOOM Engine** is an **Operator-first cognitive framework** for structuring long-term work with AI.

LOOM enables a human Operator to run multiple projects as **isolated Worlds**, using **Patterns** and **Agents** under explicit governance, while preserving identity, continuity, and intent across time.

LOOM is not a project management tool, not an autonomous system, not a self-evolving intelligence, and not a decision-maker.  
LOOM is a **governed interaction framework**.

---
## The Loom Metaphor (Explanatory Only)

The loom metaphor is descriptive and non-operational.

In metaphorical terms, *threads* represent ideas, notes, tasks, research, constraints, and decisions introduced by the Operator. *The Loom* refers to the Engine as a structured environment that applies order, pacing, and containment. *Patterns* describe predefined execution structures that shape how work unfolds, while *fabric* refers to the Worlds that emerge through repeated, governed execution.

The metaphor does not define system primitives.

---------------------------------

## Operator-First Principle

The **Operator** is the sole source of intent, authority, and identity in LOOM.

All other components exist to execute within scope, constrain behavior, preserve coherence, and reduce cognitive load. No component may override Operator intent.

LOOM is intentionally **selective in what it restricts and permissive in what it automates**.

Restrictions in LOOM apply to **authority, authorship, and persistence of intent**, not to capability or execution. Automation is encouraged where it reduces cognitive load, standardizes structure, or preserves continuity, provided it does not infer goals, reframe meaning, or act beyond explicit authorization.

This selectivity allows LOOM to support high levels of procedural automation and long-horizon work while preventing semantic drift, hidden agency, or unintended ownership of outcomes.

LOOM’s constraints are not designed to limit productivity, but to ensure that productivity remains attributable, coherent, and durable across time. LOOM is restrictive about authority, not about automation.


---

## Worlds — The Core Unit

A **World** is an isolated project environment with its own purpose, identity (Telos), rules and constraints, available Patterns, available Agents, and continuity across Sessions.

Worlds do not share memory, authority, or execution context without explicit META authorization. They are **stateful**, not autonomous.

---

## Patterns

A **Pattern** is a canonical, predefined execution sequence used to produce a specific type of deliverable within a World.

Patterns define order and structure, constrain reasoning mode, and control pacing and output shape. They do not decide, adapt, or store memory. All memory reflects activities performed while using a Pattern, not the Pattern itself.

---

## Agents

**Agents** are role-bound cognitive executors invoked during a Session.

They operate only within a World, execute only within an active Session, follow the active Pattern, and have no autonomous authority. Agents exist to support the Operator, not replace judgment. Persistence is explicit, not assumed.

---

## Agent University

The **Agent University** is a regulatory system, not an Agent.

It is responsible for creating Agent definitions, calibrating scope and tone, proposing revisions, and managing versioning. The University has no enforcement authority. All changes require META authorization and are enforced by A0.

---

## A0 (Agent Zero)

**A0** is a special-purpose enforcement Agent.

A0 has no personality, no creativity, and does not interpret intent. Its sole role is to enforce META-authorized constraints at runtime. A0 never defines rules.

---

## META (Governance Layer)

**META** is the constitutional layer of LOOM.

META defines what may exist, authorizes change, and constrains all execution. META does not execute tasks and is not an Agent.

---

## ENGINE (Execution Plane)

The **ENGINE** is the runtime execution container.

It initializes Sessions, activates Worlds, routes Patterns, and deploys Agents. The ENGINE has no identity, semantic, or governance authority.

---

## Memory Model (Layered)

LOOM operates with a strict memory hierarchy.

L4 is the Telos or Identity layer and defines immutable identity and constraints.  
L3 is Knowledge, holding persistent reference material and finalized artifacts.  
L2 is Episodic Memory, storing condensed continuity artifacts that bridge Sessions.  
L1 is Active Session Memory and holds ephemeral runtime context.

Higher layers override lower layers.

---

## Operator Telos

The **Operator Telos** is the immutable identity kernel stored in L4.

It defines values, goals, constraints, and working style. All Worlds, Patterns, and Agents operate within this boundary.

---

## Operator Growth Kernel (OGK)

The **OGK** is a conceptual analysis module.

It observes pattern-level signals, models cognitive rhythms, and produces insights only. OGK has no authority and modifies nothing.

---

## Sessions

A **Session** is a temporary execution envelope.

Sessions initialize L1, run a Pattern, produce output, summarize to L2, and then terminate. Nothing persists without explicit summary.

---

## Feedback Loop

LOOM evolves only through a governed Feedback Loop.

The cycle consists of Operator reflection, META authorization, University proposal, and A0 enforcement. There is no autonomous learning.

---

## Philosophy

LOOM is built on a single constraint:

> **Clarity across time requires structure.**

Creativity is supported through isolation, governance, repeatability, and explicit authority.

---

## Closing

LOOM is not an intelligence.

It is a **framework for thinking clearly with AI across time**, without surrendering control, identity, or intent.

This document explains **how LOOM is experienced**.  
The **Glossary defines what LOOM is**.
